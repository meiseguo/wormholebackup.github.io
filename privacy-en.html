Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 1, 2026

Thank you for using Wormhole Backup. This privacy policy explains how we handle your information.

Information Collection

This app does not collect, store, or upload any personal information. All photos and videos are transferred over a local Wi-Fi network to your own storage device. This app does not send any data to the internet or third-party servers.

Photo Library Access

This app requests access to your photo library only when you tap "Start Backup". This permission is used solely to read the photos and videos you select and transfer them to your storage device over the local network. We do not use this data for any other purpose.

Camera Access

This app requires camera access to scan QR codes for connecting to your Wormhole Backup device. We do not collect any other information through the camera.

Data Storage

All backed-up photos and videos are stored only on your own storage device (such as a USB drive or external hard drive). This app does not retain copies of backup data on your device or any server.

Data Deletion

Since this app does not collect any personal data, no deletion request is necessary. Simply unplug your storage device or format it to completely remove all backup data.

Third-Party Services

This app does not use any third-party analytics tools, advertising SDKs, or crash reporting services. This app does not initiate any internet connections.

Children's Privacy

This app does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. When we do, we will notify you within the app.

Notice for Users in the European Economic Area (EEA)

If you are located in the European Economic Area, you have the following rights:
- Right of access: You may request information about what data is processed.
- Right to erasure: Since this app does not collect any personal data, no deletion is necessary. Simply unplug your storage device to remove all data.
- Right to lodge a complaint: If you believe data processing violates GDPR, you have the right to complain to your local supervisory authority.
